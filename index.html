Welcome to the Stevenson Group

We are a highly interdisciplinary research group at Northeastern University, pushing the boundaries of quantum sensing and nanoscale physics. Our work bridges chemistry, physics, biology, and materials science to address some of the most challenging questions in modern science.

Using cutting-edge spin-based quantum sensors, we develop novel techniques for unprecedented measurement sensitivity and spatial resolution. Our research enables the study of individual molecules, quantum materials, and biological systems at scales previously thought impossible.

Located in the heart of Boston's innovation district, we collaborate with leading institutions worldwide to advance the frontiers of quantum science and its applications.

Research Areas

Exploring the quantum frontier through innovative sensing technologies

Quantum Sensing

Developing next-generation quantum sensors based on single spins, achieving unprecedented sensitivity in magnetic field detection and enabling new applications in materials characterization.

Learn more →

Nanoscale NMR

Pushing nuclear magnetic resonance to the single-molecule level, opening new possibilities for structural biology and surface chemistry at unprecedented resolution.

Learn more →

Quantum Materials

Investigating exotic quantum phases of matter using advanced spectroscopic techniques, revealing new physics in strongly correlated systems and topological materials.

Learn more →

Biophysics Applications

Applying quantum sensing to biological systems, from protein dynamics to cellular processes, bridging quantum physics with life sciences.
